About Me

I am a PhD Candidate in the Department of Mechanical Engineering and the Research Laboratory of Electronics at MIT, where I work in the Jeehwan Kim Research Group. My research focuses on scalable heterogeneous integration of semiconductors and functional materials for next-generation electronic and optoelectronic systems.

I am particularly interested in remote epitaxy, monolithic 3D integration, advanced semiconductor growth, and red InGaN micro-LEDs. My long-term goal is to develop practical integration technologies that bridge materials innovation, device engineering, and real-world hardware applications.

Research Interests

  • Heterogeneous semiconductor integration: integrating advanced materials and device platforms for future hardware systems.
  • Remote epitaxy and 2D materials: enabling strain engineering, layer release, and scalable growth pathways.
  • Monolithic 3D integration: low-temperature and growth-based integration of materials and devices.
  • Red InGaN micro-LEDs: high-indium-content growth, strain-relaxed templates, and routes toward monolithic RGB integration.
